T Tribute Availability & Lead Tool
Quick guide — take a lead, check availability, and create the HubSpot deal & Teams post in one place.
This tool turns a single set of call notes into three things: an availability answer you can give the caller, a HubSpot deal, and a Teams post — without re-typing anything. Paste your notes, review what it filled in, and submit.
One-time setup (top-right of the page):
1. Open the tool and enter the team password.
2. Click Sign in with Microsoft — this is your login and lets you post to your own Teams channels.
3. Click Connect HubSpot once — deals you create are owned by you.
1 Paste your call notes and hit Parse
In the New lead box at the top, type or paste everything the caller told you — who called, who needs care, where, the schedule, when to start, and the referral source. Then click ✨ Parse with AI. It reads the notes and fills the availability check and the whole deal form below.
2 Check Availability & read the answer
Click Check Availability. The result tells you, at a glance, exactly what to do on the call:
  • Confirm now — the days you can commit to right away.
  • Follow up with scheduling — the days (or the whole case) to hedge on.
  • Say to the caller — the exact words to use.
Need the detail (which caregivers, the day-by-day grid)? Click “▾ Show availability details.” You don’t need it on the call.
3 Review the deal, then create it & post to Teams
Scroll to Lead details. Everything is already filled from your notes — glance over it and fix anything that needs it (the AI flags anything it couldn’t place under the notes box). Pick the Team and Channel for the Teams post, then choose one of the three buttons:
  • Create HubSpot deal — deal only.
  • Post to Teams — post only.
  • Create deal & post to Teams — both at once.
A few tips
  • Always review before submitting — the AI gets dates and picklists right most of the time, but a quick glance catches the odd one (e.g. “next Tuesday”).
  • The deal is created owned by you (whoever is connected to HubSpot), and the Teams post goes out as you.
  • Setting a Deal stage like Closed-Won, Lost, or Turndown reveals the matching reason fields.
