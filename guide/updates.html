T Availability & Lead Tool — What's New
A fresh round of upgrades from your feedback. Here's what changed.
Thanks for the feedback — it shaped all of this. Quick highlights below.
📞 A simpler answer on the call
The result is built for live calls: can you confirm, the days to confirm now, the days to hedge with scheduling, and the exact words to say. The detailed grid and caregiver specifics are tucked behind "▾ Show availability details" — out of the way during the call.
✉️ New: follow-up emails, right from the tool
A Follow-up email section now drafts the note for you in Tribute's voice, from the lead details — then you review, edit, and send from your own Outlook:
  • Thank the referral source — addressed to the referral partner, recipient pre-filled.
  • Follow up with the family — a warm, personal note to the caller/family.
And it's truthful to the deal's stage — it won't say care is "starting" unless it actually is, and never invents dates or medical details.
🤝 RDMs automatically looped in on referrals
Since RDMs aren't in the tool, the moment you create a deal with a referral source, the right RDM for that market is automatically emailed a ready-to-send thank-you — including the partner's email address and a suggested subject — so they can send it on with one quick review. Nothing falls through the cracks.
📝 The deal form fills itself — more completely
  • Every lead field auto-fills from your notes — caller, client, referral, lead source, market, salesperson, and more.
  • General Summary and Details about client are now distinct (no duplicate text).
  • Added Start Date of Service; pick a Deal stage (Closed-Won / Lost / Turndown) and the matching reason fields appear.
  • Next steps only fill when the caller actually states one — no more invented info.
🗂️ New: Lead Desk — send the CSA in a couple clicks
A new Lead Desk shows your team's Qualified leads waiting on a Client Service Agreement. Pick one and the tool builds the CSA from the right regional template, pre-filled with the client's details, then opens it in DocuSign for you to review and send for signature — as you. Once it's sent, the deal automatically advances to Contract Sent. (One-time: Connect DocuSign, alongside Microsoft and HubSpot.)
⚡ Three ways to submit + your own Teams
Choose Create HubSpot deal, Post to Teams, or both at once. Sign in with Microsoft once and the Teams post goes to your own team & channel, as you — and the deal is created owned by you.
